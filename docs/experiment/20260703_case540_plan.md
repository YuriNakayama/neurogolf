# 20260703 case540 plan - parallel candidate supply expansion

Accepted Public Score:

- `7187.41`

## Background

Cases531-539 show that the submit stall is caused by candidate depletion and
stricter post-regression gating, not by a Kaggle/API outage. The target cadence
is now about `72` submits per `13` hours, but submitting below-threshold or
review-risk bundles caused recent non-improving and regressing turns.

The scoring metric is logarithmic:

- `points = max(1, 25 - ln(max(1, cost)))`
- improvement for an exact replacement is `ln(old_cost / new_cost)`

Therefore the submit queue should be filled by candidates with high relative
cost reduction, while exact low-risk micro reductions are accumulated until the
bundle is large enough to justify a submit.

## Hypothesis

Increasing independent candidate-supply workers should recover submit cadence
without lowering the correctness/risk gate. The highest-value search space is
not simply the largest ONNX files, but tasks where a structural simplification,
solver replacement, dtype/initializer reduction, or near-miss repair can produce
a meaningful cost ratio.

## Parallel workers

Spawn six non-overlapping workers:

1. Low/mid-cost non-risk mechanical ONNX simplification.
2. High-gain near-miss repair refresh.
3. Solver-bank/custom solver replacement on skipped and high-interest tasks.
4. Exact micro-candidate accumulator and bundle builder.
5. Safe dtype/initializer compression.
6. Backlog mining and submit-cadence triage.

## Gate

Submit immediately only when one of these is true:

- one exact non-risk candidate has standalone gain `>= 0.020`;
- a low-risk exact micro bundle reaches gain `>= 0.015` with at most five tasks.

Do not submit:

- hard-risk tasks;
- review-risk tasks bundled with unrelated micros;
- known blocked near-miss families unless the repair is genuinely new and exact;
- candidates with forbidden ops/functions.

## Expected output

If a candidate qualifies, build `submission.zip`, submit, wait for Public Score,
and write the result. If no candidate qualifies after the worker reports are
collected, record no-submit and carry forward the validated micro backlog.
