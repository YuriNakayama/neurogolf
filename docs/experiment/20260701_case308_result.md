# 20260701 case308 result - reject task324 smaller kernel correction

## Result

No ONNX change and no submit.

`task324` smaller-kernel variants were reproduced, but no cheap correction was
identified.

## Baseline

Current `task324.onnx`:

- params: 1290
- memory: 7308
- cost: 8598
- `n_fail=0`

## Probe

Reproduced the case236 kernel trims:

| variant | cost | n_pass | n_fail | conclusion |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| 33x33 kernel | 8462 | 264 | 2 | incorrect and still above 2% gate |
| 31x31 kernel | 8334 | 261 | 5 | clears 2% cost gate but incorrect |
| 29x29 kernel | 8214 | 253 | 13 | incorrect |

The 31x31 failures are only seven cells across five arc-gen examples. They are
all boundary cells at the far end of long diagonal propagation lines.

## Why rejected

The 31x31 trim saves 264 params, leaving roughly 95 cost budget before it loses
the 2% advantage. Correcting the missing boundary cells would require a general
extra propagation rule for long diagonals. That is unlikely to fit under 95 cost
without reintroducing the larger kernel behavior that was trimmed away.

The 33x33 trim is even less attractive: it saves only 136 params and remains
above the 2% target before any correction.

## Conclusion

Keep the adopted 35x35 kernel from case236. The smaller kernels are close but
not profitable under the ratio gate unless a very compact boundary extension is
found.
